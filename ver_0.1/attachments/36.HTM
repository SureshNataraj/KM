Transactional License Program

Volume licensing made easy

The Transactional License Program (TLP) makes it easy for organizations of all sizes to get the benefits of volume purchasing through Adobe® Open Options. This program is available worldwide and covers most Adobe products. There are no contracts to sign and no need to forecast future software purchases. Your pricing is based on the size of your transaction. For every purchase of a new eligible Adobe software license, upgrade of a previously acquired license or enrollment of an eligible license in the Maintenance program, you will receive points. There is a 20-point minimum to participate in the Transactional License Program. The more you buy, the greater your discount level.

In addition, when you purchase products through the TLP, Adobe provides you with an easy-to-use, online licensing history that simplifies software administration and record keeping. This custom site provides your organization with a single location for up-to-date access to your comprehensive Adobe software licensing information.

The TLP is open to corporate, government, education, and small-business customers.

Previously acquired licenses are defined as either licenses from shrinkwrapped purchases or those acquired through licensing programs.

A TLP license includes a serial number and certificate specifying the licensee, the covered product (or products), order date, and the total number of copies available for installation.


Discount rates are determined based on the number of points in the transaction. The license of most Adobe software products accumulates either one or two points. The number of points accumulated is the same for new purchases, upgrades from a previous version, or enrollment of a product in the Maintenance program.


As the number of points increases in a single purchase transaction, so does the discount. Discounts are provided in the following six levels.

Maintenance

Maintenance is like an upgrade insurance program. When you purchase Maintenance for a product, you are entitled to all enhancements, bug fixes, updates, and upgrades. The term of a Maintenance program is two years, plus the number of days that are required to complete the calendar quarter. For example, if you enroll a product in Maintenance on 10/21/2002, the Maintenance coverage will expire on 12/31/2004. Adobe will automatically send notification of available upgrades to TLP Maintenance customers.
